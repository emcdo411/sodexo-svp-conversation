## 🧱 Model Alignment

### Sodexo

![Sodexo Align](https://img.shields.io/badge/alignment-partial-E24B4A?style=flat-square)

* Strong governance architecture
* Clear policy and oversight structures
* Recognized leadership in data and AI

**Gap relative to model:**

* Governance not fully embedded in workflows
* Operator-level ownership not consistently defined
* Feedback loops may not be enforced at execution level

**Resulting friction:**

```text id="sodexo-friction"
Governance Strength → Execution Bottleneck → Slower Operational Impact
```

---

### Compass

![Compass Align](https://img.shields.io/badge/alignment-partial-2D8A6B?style=flat-square)

* AI embedded in a significant portion of operations
* Faster decision velocity at the edge
* Strong execution orientation

**Gap relative to model:**

* Governance may lag behind deployment scale
* Criteria validity risk as systems expand
* Trust calibration may vary across environments

**Resulting friction:**

```text id="compass-friction"
Deployment Speed → Governance Exposure → Potential Drift Risk
```

---

## 🔍 Key Insight

![Insight](https://img.shields.io/badge/insight-neither_is_complete-E24B4A?style=for-the-badge)

> Sodexo has the structure.
> Compass has the motion.

Neither fully satisfies the **operations-sponsored governance model**.

---

## 🎯 Strategic Opportunity

![Opportunity](https://img.shields.io/badge/opportunity-bridge_the_gap-2D8A6B?style=for-the-badge)

The advantage does not come from choosing one model over the other.

It comes from **integrating both**:

* Embedding governance into operational workflows
* Enforcing feedback loops at the operator level
* Establishing clear ownership of AI-driven decisions
